# Ryan Law, our Director of Content at Ahrefs, built a fully automated Claude Code workflow that creates publish-ready blog articles in just 8 mins.

Ryan Law, our Director of Content at Ahrefs, built a fully automated Claude Code workflow that creates publish-ready blog articles in just 8 mins.

It runs on 23 custom skill files + Ahrefs MCP.

Ryan has already published 15 articles on the Ahrefs Blog with this workflow and updated ~30 more.

So I invited him back on the Ahrefs Podcast (his 4th appearance) to walk through his entire AI workflow step by step, while I (gently) grilled him on every detail.

Here's what we covered:

🔸 Why obsessing over the perfect drafting prompt is the wrong place to focus.
🔸 Why you should never compress your writing voice into a bullet list of rules.
🔸 Why every step of your AI workflow needs to save its output.
🔸 Why "AI slop" mostly comes from skipping the steps that matter.
🔸 Why Ryan calls his own pipeline "overengineered."
🔸 Why every writer on the team has their own forked version of the pipeline.

---

## Annotation

**Claim:** A production-grade AI content workflow requires 23+ discrete skill files, per-writer forked pipelines, and mandatory output saves at every step — "AI slop" is not an AI problem but a workflow design problem caused by skipping the steps that enforce quality.

**Evidence type:** case-study

**Playbook implication:** For B2B SaaS content teams, an AI production workflow is not a single prompt or template — it is a multi-step pipeline with discrete skill files for each phase, per-writer customization, and enforced output checkpoints; the investment is in workflow engineering, not prompt refinement, and quality comes from not skipping steps.
