# AI search traffic to 75k websites dropped from 2.9M to 2.8M over the past 11 months.

AI search traffic to 75k websites dropped from 2.9M to 2.8M over the past 11 months. That's a 3% decline while AI adoption is at an all-time high.

[see yourself at 👉 chatgpt-vs-google(.)com]

And I don't think we're going to see much traffic growth from AI search going forward.

AI chatbots are walled gardens. They're designed to keep you inside the chat, not send you somewhere else.

Which brings me to a bigger problem for us marketers.

How do you actually measure your performance in AI search?

Because if your website's referral traffic from AI is going down, it doesn't mean your brand stopped being mentioned. AI chatbots can talk about you all day without ever sending a single click to your website.

And even bot traffic from AI crawlers isn't a reliable signal. When ChatGPT mentions your brand, that mention might be coming from a third-party website, not yours.

So the only way to actually know how you're doing in AI search is to consistently track whether you show up for the prompts that matter to your business.

This definitely comew with its own limitations and caveats. But right now, 
there's nothing better.
