# The SEO industry just changed overnight with the Google I/O event, and the search interface has changed overnight with it.

The SEO industry just changed overnight with the Google I/O event, and the search interface has changed overnight with it.

The image below was created by Google in its search box.

I asked Google what had changed in search overnight, then asked it to create a graphic to show the changes visually... all within the search interface.

This is basically ChatGPT in search. 

And yet we had everyone from Google itself to leading SEO influencers telling you 'it's just SEO'.

No, it isn't.

This is all about AI recommending your brand in buying situations.

Call it GEO, AI SEO, AEO, search marketing, brand visibility or anything else you like, but it's NOT SEO.

Not how it's been sold and delivered for the last 20 years.

Different strategies, different objectives, different deliverables, different outcomes.

We've had leading SEO influencers and consultants on stage, grifting their Substacks and repeatedly saying "it's just SEO" without offering practical, real-world advice on how it's just SEO or what to do.

Me? I've spent the last 2 years writing about this and telling people what to do.

Build brand mentions.
Earn contextually relevant links about your brand.
Build brand co-occurrences related to what you do.
Define the performance attributes of your brand's products or services.
Create owned content that promotes these.
Get people talking about what you do and the problems you solve.
Aim to trigger preferential attachment.
Aim for fame.

And we built the tools for the job.

OptiGen is our page audit tool, and nothing exists like it in the world.
It analyses your page from a GEO and SEO basis, giving real-world advice to ensure your pages perform better in both traditional and generative search environments.

And then we built the F.A.M.E Engine.

Our tool that maps and analyses media mentions and brand co-occurrences to see how the media is talking about you.

It also tracks your AI visibility, analyses citation sources, and much more.

Those who have followed me and listened to me over the last 2 years on this are prepared, or are quickly getting prepared, for the changes.

I know because people message me on this.

However, 90% of what's being written on AI search is nonsense.
It's SEO consultants who spend most of their time repeating "It's just SEO" 

At the same time, being strategically ambiguous and saying things like "create helpful content" and "focus on E-E-A-T".

The reason they say these things is that over the last 2 years, saying them has served them well. 

It's been an echo chamber and a fast track to more likes, more followers and more subscribers.

But it's also massively damaged the search industry.

People buy certainty.
When a marketing channel looks uncertain, they simply wait for consensus to emerge.

As I've been saying for years, your SEO gurus will not be your GEO gurus.

They simply don't get it.
