# Another Example of GEO Advice in Action:

Another Example of GEO Advice in Action:

More screenshots from our bespoke GEO and SEO Audit tool, OptiGen. This time I've run it on a London-based solicitor whose site is optimised for 2 core keywords, 'Chelsea Conveyancing Solicitors | London Solicitors'.

This site scores 68% optimisation for SEO and 62% for GEO.

So, what could it be doing better in GEO?

Examples of things it's doing well:

The site clearly targets local homebuyers, property investors, and commercial lessees in SW6.

Evidence
"Whether you are buying your first home, investing in a buy-to-let, or managing a property portfolio..."

Recommendation to improve.
"Explicitly mention high-net-worth individuals or specific commercial sectors to sharpen the audience profile."

The areas to improve quickly are:

Scale/Stage Fit
60/100

Confidence:
Medium
Vague on the scale of commercial conveyancing they can handle. 'Disposals & investments' is broad.

Evidence
"COMMERCIAL CONVEYANCING Leases, Lease extensions, grants, renewals, disposals & investments."

Recommendation to improve
"Specify the transaction sizes or types of commercial properties (e.g., retail units, office spaces) they typically handle."

And.

Methodology
40/100

Confidence:
Low
No methodology is explained. 'Act quickly and efficiently' is a generic claim without process backing.

Evidence
"Our conveyancing solicitors act quickly and efficiently to protect your interests at every stage of the process."

Recommendation to fix:
"Add a 'Our 4-Step Conveyancing Process' section to provide a structured, extractable methodology."

OptiGen has identified over 50 opportunities to improve SEO and GEO performance for this site; most are fixes that can be implemented in minutes.

What's even better is that our tool also identifies which content you should create to reinforce your core performance attributes and increase sales and revenue from AI, traditional search and social media.

## Annotation

**Claim:** Pages that score well for SEO often fail GEO because they lack explicit methodology, precise audience specificity, and structured extractable processes — generic claims like "act quickly and efficiently" score low because AI systems cannot extract a concrete, citable answer from them.

**Evidence type:** case-study

**Playbook implication:** For B2B SaaS content production, every page needs a GEO pass distinct from SEO — specifically: named methodology sections, explicit audience and use-case specificity, and structured process descriptions (e.g., "Our 4-Step X Process") that AI systems can extract and cite as concrete evidence rather than marketing generalities.
