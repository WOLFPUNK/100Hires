# We got some early data from @Similarweb on user behavior in AI Mode vs. classic search.

We got some early data from @Similarweb on user behavior in AI Mode vs. classic search. 100k users from 5/20/25 to 6/19/25.

Although user adoption is low (only 2%) early patterns are emerging...

The disparity in referral traffic between the two channels is immense, but the average page views for referral traffic from AI Mode is higher than that of classic search and it is climbing rapidly.

---

## Annotation

**Claim:** AI Mode referral traffic is a small fraction of classic search volume (2% adoption), but users who do click through from AI Mode view significantly more pages per visit than classic search referrals — and that engagement gap is widening.

**Evidence type:** data

**Playbook implication:** For B2B SaaS, AI Mode referrals are low volume but high intent — the content experience after the click matters more than for classic search, because these visitors are further into a research chain and will engage with more of the site. Content architecture (internal linking, depth, related content) becomes a higher-leverage investment for AI Mode traffic than for organic.
