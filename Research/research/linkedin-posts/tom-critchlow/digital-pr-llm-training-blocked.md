# What are the implications for digital PR when all the top media sites block LLMs from training on their data?

What are the implications for digital PR when all the top media sites block LLMs from training on their data?

54% of the top media sites now block Google-Extended. Which means any coverage in these outlets isn't making it into the training set.

So if you're working in digital PR and reporting back wins in terms of coverage - hoping that it will "influence the models" you should be mindful of the publisher/llm dynamics at play right now.

Sure - most publisher content can still appear in query fan out searches but it's not the same thing as influencing the base model.....
