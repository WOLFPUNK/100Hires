# Anyone can build almost anything now.

Anyone can build almost anything now.
Vibe coding has made it possible to spin up real solutions in hours. 
 
The problem it creates is that if everyone can build, the bottleneck shifts entirely to getting noticed. The noise is louder than ever, and most products will drown in it unless they are created by founders with platforms. 

AI can handle some of this. There are tools that will autonomously:
- Implement SEO best practices on your landing page
- Set up and launch paid campaigns with minimal input
- A/B test copy and optimize for conversions

That's real, useful automation which removes grunt work.

What it can't do is figure out why anyone should care about your product in the first place. It can't find the unexpected angle that makes something click. It can't build the kind of trust that turns curious visitors into loyal users.
Driving real adoption is still a creative problem because it requires understanding people, not just patterns.

 The pool of marketers who can solve these problems isn't growing at the same rate as the apps that will need them. 

I'm more bullish than ever on smart humans in marketing, and the leverage they have right now is enormous.
