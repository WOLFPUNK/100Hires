# For those of you following along, we started an AEO experiment using Clearscope's own platform

For those of you following along, we started an AEO experiment using Clearscope's own platform to test whether a repeatable content process can earn brand mentions in AI-generated responses. Our target prompt: "Answer Engine Optimization."

This is our first check-in, about 3 weeks later.

Here's what's happening.

On our strongest prompt — "tools for tracking AI citations and brand mentions in LLMs" — Clearscope's brand mention rate has climbed to over 38% in just 3 weeks, up from 0% when we started. That movement came directly from publishing content that targets the exact queries Gemini uses to construct its answer.

That's the methodology working in real time. 

So next we asked ourselves: why haven't the other two prompts moved?

This is where it gets really interesting. On both remaining prompts, it's not just Clearscope that isn't showing up. Even the biggest, most established players in the space are barely registering. Semrush and Ahrefs, who dominate nearly every tool-related query we track, are getting single-digit or low double-digit visibility on these prompts.

That's the real signal. When no brand is getting meaningful traction, it means the prompt intent is wrong. Gemini isn't interpreting these as "recommend a tool" questions. It's treating them as educational queries and returning tactical content instead of brand recommendations. That's not a content problem you can write your way out of. It's a prompt selection problem. And catching it early is one of the most valuable things Prompt Tracking does. 

So naturally the next question is: where do we go from here?

We will continue to track the two prompts that haven't moved. And at the same time we've identified a replacement prompt. It's one where Clearscope is already showing up at 25%, but competitors are dominating: Semrush at 98%, Surfer at 85%, Ahrefs at 85%.

That's actually the ideal starting point. We already have a foothold. The question is whether targeted content can close the gap. We're going to find out.

Update coming in a few weeks.
