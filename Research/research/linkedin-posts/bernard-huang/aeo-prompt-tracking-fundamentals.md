# If SEO = tracking keywords, then AEO = tracking prompts.

If SEO = tracking keywords, then AEO = tracking prompts. The problem with prompt tracking is multi-fold:

1. There is no definitive source of prompt volume
When AI Mode / ChatGPT ads become more apparent, I think marketers will likely get better tooling to understand volumes. Whether it's impressions on a topic, volumes on broad modifiers (how many prompts include "X" or "Y"), or something else...

I think the best way to look at prompt volumes for now is looking at question modifiers in Google autocomplete + people also ask and pairing those questions with search volume data from Google Ads. Video below to help explain this more 👇 

shoutout Kathryn Arthur

2. The volatility of a response
Whether it's from the AI models temperature or usage of memory/context, personalization will happen and influence a response. That said, I don't think the overall influence due to volatility is that crazy (for now). In early experiments at Clearscope, we're finding that prompt mentions/citations tend to normalize over repetitive prompting.

The key here is to gather enough data, and look at responses as PERCENTAGES rather than absolutes.

3. It's still so early
Will AI Mode be released in the form that we know it? How will Siri's model differ from Google's model? Is ChatGPT even going to be relevant in 1-2 years?

Investing in something when the sand is still shifting is scary. And it's tough to say how everything will shake out.

However, I believe that AI will be around and replace traditional search. So better start experimenting on how to win better AI visibility so that you can set yourself up for success!
