# You might have noticed that AI models do not treat every type of website exactly the same way when putting together answers.

Recognized consumer brands get mentioned in the output quite frequently. The AI simply names them outright without always feeling the need to explicitly link them as a source.

A different pattern emerges for publishers and content aggregators. They are often cited as reference material but rarely mentioned by name. For example, Medium was cited 16 times in the Semrush data I recently analyzed, but not named once.

For businesses whose core value relies on being recognized as an information authority, this presents an interesting dynamic to navigate going forward.

---

## Annotation

**Claim:** AI models name recognized brands in outputs without citing them as sources, while publishers are cited as references but rarely named — meaning brand authority and citation authority are distinct forms of AI visibility that require different strategies.
